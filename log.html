lady bird (2017) #movie

white woman movie. i’m sorry but i truly cannot see what all the hype was about. like, it was very relatable, even as a poc, about the relationship with the mother, but nothing happened??? and i get that it was part of the point to be a naturalistic movie, but i finished watching it and felt like i just had my time wasted. and as a period piece—i didn’t catch that it was a period piece at first because it did NOT do a good job of setting the era—it didn’t fully utilize it’s backdrop and felt like it was set in the past just because the director was partially writing it as a memoir. ultimately disappointing because i had heard people razz it up like it was the greatest thing since the vvitch

frankenweenie (2012) #movie

short, sweet, and very simple. not to give racist tim burton any praise, but i loved the concept. the choice to do black-and-white stop motion was perfect because it made everyone in this strangeville-esque town look so creepy and gothic in addition to their purple prose ass dialogue. what i disliked most is the way it just suddenly… ends. like, sparky the dog is brought back a second time and that’s literally it. it seems like such a week ending for an otherwise decent movie

five nights at freddy's (2023) #movie

before i start, let me just say that i never played fnaf when it first came out—i watched markiplier play every single mainline instalment, but that’s as far as i know about the lore™️. it was an enjoyable viewing experience—i liked it, but i didn’t love it. all the acting was fantastic, even the children, and the cameos were well integrated even the living tombstone’s music. i especially ADORED the opening scene, but the movie never quite recaptured that same feeling akin to the first game. so i understood where this movie was coming from in trying to appeal to the fans but also attract newcomers, and as someone learning screenwriting adaptations are really hard, BUT i have to say that in trying not tie itself down too much to the games’ plots, the final product was somewhat lacking depth. what i mean by that is we are exposited about mike’s backstory with his brother without much reasoning as to why it important until the afton reveal at the climax. vanessa (vanny the rabbit from security breach i think?) is revealed as afton’s daughter in a scene with a horrendous photoshopped polaroid, something changed from the games, but throughout the movie she claims to have tried to stop mike from incurring the animatronics’ wrath… this isn’t true, like at all? she comes on strong and tells him all sorts of things about the pizzeria, stares creepily at a wall of images, and then does the typical miscommunication thing of not explaining why he needs to stay away. it’s really annoying because she has little purpose in the story aside from exposition and fan service: but if you’re a fan it’s not that bad because i think she was cast really well. next, abby’s whole “i see dead kids” thing made less than zero sense because, *why*? why is she able to see these kids for, presumably, a long time before mike got the pizzeria job? was it because afton killed their brother? she wasn’t even born then. as an aside, it’s never made clear what happened to mike and abby’s parents. it’s also not quite clear what happened to the aunt after the freddy animatronic killed her, nor what she does about the hooligans she sent to trash the pizzeria in the beginning of the movie after trying to get dirt on mike. a few points like this are just sorta dropped in favour of focusing on the pizzeria. and on that note, let me bring up my biggest gripe with the movie: it’s not even five nights!! okay that’s a lie, i double checked, and it *technically* is five nights. my real biggest gripe is that it feels more like a family drama with fnaf as a backdrop rather than a fnaf story due to how the fnaf elements are handled. i really wish the animatronics revealed themselves to mike BEFORE abby came in as i would have loved some sense of the anxiety from the first game—not necessarily echoing the gameplay, but more of a callback to having to work as an actual security guard instead of just sleeping the shift away. because for three of the five nights, mike literally just sleeps and his dream was not interesting to watch. genuinely, most of the runtime is spent in mike’s dream rather than focusing on the titular pizzeria. i hope the fans, both young and old, who watched it liked it, and i hope the sequels get made. i just wish it was more five nights at freddy’s than it ended up being

voice of a thousand years by Fawaz al-Matrouk

another banger of a short story, this time a medieval sci-fi steampunk tale steeped in islamic mythology and science that does not eschew faith. the blend of science and spirituality in the story was so seamless, because so often people toss aside the place religion can have in sci-fi, which is especially poignant when it comes to muslim polymaths whose contributions form the basis for many of our modern scientific feats (robotics, math, chemistry). i really liked how david was clear to the reader as an alien of some sort, but to ibn hashem, he is more akin to a jinn, viewing the creature in the qanuj through the lens of his spirituality. even though ibn hashem is less faithful than his son, he still does all his prayers, he still reads the quran and never loses his faith because it and his science are not incompatible. the way david described his perception of the world and movement through objects by reshaping atoms (which i did not know is an idea over 1000 years old!) was so cool to read, a truly alien way of life that aided the sci-fi aspect of the story.

held (2021) #movie

not bad. the social commentary was very apt but i fear it played its hand too heavily during the reveal with all the dead eyed “submissive” women whom the project successfully “trained” (blegh). not to say the voice itself was any less heavy handed but i did prefer its brand of obey/be subservient to the in your face PSA during the climactic reveal. all in all, i found it a decent watch and the main character incredibly sympathetic for her anxiety and understandable fight/flight/freeze/fawn response
